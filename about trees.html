About Trees

Trees are an essential part of our ecosystem. They have been around for hundreds of millions of years and come in thousands of different species. From towering redwoods to small bonsai trees, they play a crucial role in maintaining life on Earth.

Why Trees are Important

  • Oxygen Production: Leaves pull in carbon dioxide and water, and use the energy of the sunto convert this into chemical compounds, giving off oxygen as a by-product.
  • Climate Control: By absorbing carbon dioxide, trees help to mitigate the greenhouse effect and slow down climate change.
  • Wildlife Habitat: A single tree can be home to hundreds of species of insects, fungi, moss, mammals, and plants.
  • Decidious Trees Deciduous trees are woody plants that shed their leaves annually, typically before winter or a dry season.

Types of Decidious Trees

Maple

(Acer): Famous for their deeply lobed leaves, vibrant autumn colors, and winged seeds. Popular species include the Sugar Maple and the Japanese Maple.

Oak

(Quercus): Known for their strength, grandeur, and longevity, oaks produce lobed leaves and acorns. Common varieties include the English Oak and the Red Oak.

Birch

(Betula): Instantly recognizable by their peeling, bright white or silvery bark and delicate, triangular leaves that turn bright yellow in the fall.

Sweet Gum

(Liquidambar): Prized for their star-shaped leaves and spectacular, multi-colored autumn foliage that transitions from vibrant yellow to deep purple.

Poplar & Aspen

(Populus): Fast-growing trees with rounded leaves that "quiver" in the wind. They provide beautiful, bright yellow foliage during autumn.
