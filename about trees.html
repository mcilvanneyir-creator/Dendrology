About Trees

Trees are an essential part of our ecosystem. They have been around for hundreds of millions of years and come in thousands of different species. From towering redwoods to small bonsai trees, they play a crucial role in maintaining life on Earth.

Why Trees are Important

  • Oxygen Production: Leaves pull in carbon dioxide and water, and use the energy of the sun to convert this into chemical compounds, giving off oxygen as a by-product.
  • Climate Control: By absorbing carbon dioxide, trees help to mitigate the greenhouse effect and slow down climate change.
  • Wildlife Habitat: A single tree can be home to hundreds of species of insects, fungi, moss, mammals, and plants.
